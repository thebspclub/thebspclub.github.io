    info_node
    Half-Life info entity
    description

    Info_node entities are a critical element of Half-Life's monster AI; specifically, navigation. In order to get your monsters to pursue the player, your level must contain a graph consisting of several (sometimes a hundred or more) of these entities.

    usage

    It is quite simple to setup the info_node path AI.

    1. Select the Entity tool on the MapTools toolbar.
    2. In the New Objects dialog, pick the info_node entity.
    3. With the mouse, left-click in a 2D window and press enter.
    4. Choose the Selection tool on the MapTools toolbar and select the info_node entity.
    5. Move the info_node around each of the 2D windows until it is in the correct position.
    When info_node entities are detected in a level, the Half-Life engine will automatically build a node graph for the level. Building the graph may take a few seconds depending on the speed of your computer and the number of info_node entities in a level. This will only need to be done once, as the game engine will save this graph for future use. If the level itself is changed, the old node graph will be thrown out and a new one will be generated.

    Placement Tips

    The goal of node graphs is to give monsters the shortest, most efficient path to a goal�usually the player. However, since the player has the ability to move around the level in a variety of ways, you must place enough info_nodes so that the monster can reach the player under a variety of circumstances. In other words, you need to guarantee that the monster can reach the player regardless of the path the player takes. To do this, you must strike a balance between placing too few nodes, which will cause your monsters to get "stuck" and placing too many nodes, which will decrease the level's performance.

    Here are a few guidelines for placing info_nodes.

    General

    • Monsters cannot navigate through architecture. To keep monsters from getting stuck, never place any info_node closer than 24 units to a wall, pillar, or other piece of architecture, as measured from the center of the info_node entity. When a monster is moving around using nodes, it will try to move the center of its body over each node in the path. Since the narrowest monster is 32 units wide, it needs an absolute minimum of 16 units of clearance on each side of its body; 24 units is better. When placing larger monsters (Bullsquid, Alien Grunt), provide a minimum of 40 units of clearance in each direction from the center of the node. As a general rule, never place an info_node in a place where any part of its yellow box representation overlaps architecture. The default bounding box for info_nodes is 48 x 48 x 8 units.
    • Info_nodes may overlap each other and may be placed very close to or under monsters.
    • Place each info_node as high off the ground as you like. The engine will automatically drop them down to 8 world units from the ground when it builds the node graph.
    • Do not place info_nodes on, under, or inside func_breakables, func_pushables or any other brush entity. The exception to this rule is func_wall entities which, when used as walls or floors, may have nodes placed on them..
    • If you are planning a battle using monsters that have "take cover" behavior (grunts, houndeyes), place info_nodes on all sides of structures that make good cover, including pillars, low walls, crates, barrels, etc.
    • Be careful to leave enough vertical clearance for your monsters when placing info_nodes under structures like ducts and catwalks.
    • Assassins use a lot of info_nodes. They need roughly two to three times the nodes that other monsters need to behave correctly.
    • The node graph will NOT write out successfully if a single node can see more than 128 other nodes.
    Hallways

    • Always try to place info_nodes in the center of a hallway. Obvious exceptions are hallways with some type of obstacle (crates, pillars, etc.) in or very near the center. Again, you must leave enough side-to-side clearance for the monster to pass.
    • Place an info_node at the center of hallway intersections and corners.
    • If the hallway is very long, place several info_nodes in a straight line along the length of the hallway. A good rule of thumb is to place a node every 256 world units. This helps insure that your monster will be able to detect the next node in the graph.
    • At corners, place three nodes around the corner as illustrated below.This will allow human opponents to use the corner as cover and to jump out from time to time to take shots at the player.

    Rooms

    • First, place an info_node at every important spot in the room. Important spots are those where a player may try to hide from a monster, places for a monster to run for cover, etc.
    • Next, place an info_node just inside the room near every exit. This will help monsters get into and out of the room.
    • Next, try to visualize the action that will unfold in this room. Add info_nodes that will complete paths between the various important spots in the room, and from these spots to the exits. More nodes give monsters a better chance of finding a good path to a spot in a crowded room but too many nodes will erode your performance.
    • Add nodes slowly, and retest until the path works the way you'd like. You may receive the following error message in the console: "No path from X to Y" (where X is the number of the node nearest the monster and Y is the number of the node the monster is trying to find). The engine is telling you that it cannot build a path for the monster between these two nodes. To adjust the placement of the nodes, check the .nrp file in valve/maps/graphs. This file will tell you the location of the faulty nodes. Remember that Worldcraft will automatically reposition the height of your nodes, so use the x and y coordinates only to locate the node in question.
    Stairs/Ramps

    • In order for monsters to be able to walk up and down staircases, you must place a node on the center of the top and bottom stairs and on every other stair in the staircase.
    • If the steps in the staircase are particularly shallow or deep, you may need to move the nodes backward or forward accordingly. Remember, monsters can only find nodes they can see.
    Doors

    • When you have two spaces connected by a door, place one node on each side of the door. If a monster needs to pass through a door, it will open the door when it is standing on the node just outside the door. The engine is able to discern which monsters have the ability to open doors (those with arms!) and which don't.
    • Be sure to place the nodes far enough from rotating and other moving doors so that the door doesn't hit the monster while the monster is waiting for it to open!
    Thank you to Steve "Wedge" Bond for the above information on info_node entities.
