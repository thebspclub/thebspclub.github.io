    MechMod 1.0 Released
    [Mon Nov 22, 2004 / 10:35am PST] Kevin 'spect3r' Foster - comments (5)

    The MechMod team released Alpha version 1.0 on November 20th. Sorry for the delay on getting this to you. I have been absent for a few days.

    Mechmod brings something new to the Half Life engine. It is an alpha release simply due to the fact they will be utilizing the source engine for their next release. However, it has been in development under the Half-Life engine for a few years now.

    The mod has some very impressive features such as OpenGL based water effects, a brand new particle engine and some very dynamic game play.

    After playing this mod, you will most likely get the impression that the Half-Life engine is still keeping up to par with some of the modern engines we see today!

    Go to their download page to get it.

    Again, sorry for my delay in reporting this news. I have been.. busy.. with other Half-Life related activities.

    Tuesday November 16, 2004

    Spirit of Half-Life 1.4
    [Tue Nov 16, 2004 / 01:15pm PST] Chris 'autolycus' Bokitch - comments (9)

    Releases all around! Just got this note from Laurie Cheers:

    Spirit of Half-Life version 1.4 is out!

    This time around the release is a community effort, headed by ytiAdmin and Confused. Respect to these guys.

    Feature highlights:
    An ambient_fmodstream entity, capable of playing .mp3, .wma and .ogg files.

    env_particles can now be multiply instanced - for all you people who like to make streams of water/goo/blood/fire spurting out of bullet holes.

    weapons and ammo can now have a 'master' to stop them being picked up (perfect for tempting people from behind thin-walled gun cabinets),

    trigger_onsight can now look from multiple entities of the same name, and no longer looks from dead monsters (you could have 3 patrolling grunts, and activate something whenever one of them sees you),

    The model mirror entity... which does exactly what it says on the tin.

    Not to mention loads of new demo levels and documentation. And that's just the tip of the iceberg!

    --
    Laurie Cheers
    [Spirit of Half-Life / general]

    Friday November 5, 2004

    Source SDK Tools Available Now
    [Fri Nov 05, 2004 / 02:46pm PST] Valve Source SDK - comments (48)

    Valve has released the Source Software Development Kit (SDK) Tools via Steam. A precursor to the release of the full SDK, which will be released shortly after Half-Life 2 is made available, the Source SDK Tools release offers a comprehensive toolset for starting production on Source-based MODs, including Hammer, XSI EXP for Half-Life 2, compiling tools, the Source Model Viewer, documentation on programming, modeling, building materials, and more. For more information, please visit http://www.valve-erc.com/srcsdk/.
    [Source / documentation]
