    Xen Rebels & Xamurabi
    [Thu Jul 03, 2003 / 02:19pm PDT] Chris 'autolycus' Bokitch - comments (3)

    I received a note from Karl Jones regarding the Xen Rebels project --

    The Xen Rebels project is splitting into two separate mods -- multiplayer and singleplayer -- for migration to Half-Life 2.

    * Xamurabi will be a multiplayer semi-coop mod, combining resource management principles (inspired by the classic text-based game, Hamurabi) with a code of laws system involving player status (king, ally, neutral, enemy) and relationships between players. The goal is to found a colony in Xen ... and survive.

    * Xen Rebels remains the name of the singpleplayer mod. Xen Rebels will consist of a small camouflaged base, deep in Xen Space. This base serves as the platform for various missions, each mission by a different mapper or group of mappers.

    Work on Xen Rebels for Half-Life 1 has been suspended. We will migrate to the Half-Life 2 platform as soon as possible -- in less than a month after the SDK ships, I hope ... :)

    For further informationj, and to get on the mailing list!

    Looking at the website, I also see this very very good news: "YES -- there will be a Half-Life 2 Almanac. Count on it." Good stuff!
    [Xen Rebels / general]

    Wednesday July 2, 2003

    Half-Life 2 Modding and the SDK
    [Wed Jul 02, 2003 / 11:37am PDT] Chris 'autolycus' Bokitch - comments (14)

    As many people have figured out (judging from my email), I am helping communicate with the mod community on all subjects related to the Half-Life 2 / Source SDK and Half-Life 2 modding (or as Gabe has titled me, I am Lord of SDK Distribution).

    As my first act of lordship, I will be bequeathing... er... I'm going to post a Half-Life 2 Mod FAQ in the next couple days that contains answers to many of the common questions we've been getting. Hopefully this will slow down some of the email, and at the very least I will have something to point people towards. :)

    The answers in this FAQ will occasionally differ somewhat from what you've already seen in public. All answers in this FAQ will have been looked over by people at Valve (relevant to the specific questions) and OK'd prior to going public, so this FAQ should be pretty accurate.

    One of the most common things I've been seeing is requests for access to the SDK. We are not distributing the SDK prior to general public release. Although comments have been made to the contrary, to my knowledge only internal teams have access to the code. We understand that there is a great deal of anticipation from the community, and we want to put the necessary time and resources into the SDK so it is done right.

    Look for the FAQ here in the next couple days!
    [Half-Life 2 / general]
