    Tuesday April 5, 2005

    Hostile Planet Showcase
    [Tue Apr 05, 2005 / 12:01pm PDT] Kevin 'spect3r' Foster - comments (5)

    Hostile Planet has released two teaser videos showcasing some of the impressive features that will appear in their mod.

    Hostile Planet is an MMOFPS/RPG modification of the Half-Life 2 engine. Rival groups of players battle for control of the planet's resources to better themselves and their organization.

    I had the chance to quickly interview Helk, the project leader of HP, regarding some of the details of surrounding this project.

      First, I feel there is a slight misunderstanding as to the nature of HP, While there will be static zones such as cities and other special areas, most of the combat will take place in instanced zones (Not in the WoW sense) known as "Expeditions". We plan to keep on adding Maps & Zones to HP frequently so there is always fresh terrain to battle on.



    Helk advised that the mod only started production in mid January of 2005, which signals that this is a dedicated and hard working team. The initial concept of HP began long before Half-Life 2 was released.

    Hostile Planet is also aggressively seeking serious talent to join their team. This includes concept artists, experienced level designers, character skinners, character modellers and general texture artists.

    Go to the Hostile Planet site to view the teaser videos and get more information regarding this promising looking mod.

    "Lock & Load" - SOFTIMAGE|XSI Mod Tool Challenge
    [Tue Apr 05, 2005 / 08:51am PDT] Kevin 'spect3r' Foster - comments (0)

    From Softimage comes an announcement of a contest dubbed "Lock & Load" whereby participants have a chance of winning over $25,000 in prizes.

    Avid Computer Graphics is running the contest and has sponsors such as Hewlett-Packard, NVIDIA and GameDev.net on board.

      We are looking for original, compelling characters produced with the XSI Mod Tool that are both visually stunning and believably animated. The characters will be judged in-game on specific visual and animation criteria.

    Unfortunately, only residents in the U.S., France, Germany, the United Kingdom, or Canada (except Quebec) are eligible for this contest.

      Judges must be able to compile the XSI Mod Tool scenes (.exp) successfully as multiplayer characters for either: Half-Life 2: Deathmatch, CounterStrike: Source, or Unreal Tournament 2004.

      The Contest begins on April 4, 2005, and ends on July 1, 2005. Contest entries that are not received or postmarked on or before July 1, 2005 will be deemed ineligible.

    Head on over to the Softimage|XSI contest page for more information and contest rules.

    Wednesday March 23, 2005

    G.H.O.S.T. OPs Ver 1.1 Patch Released
    [Wed Mar 23, 2005 / 04:58pm PST] Kevin 'spect3r' Foster - comments (3)

    The G.H.O.S.T. team have released a patch that addresses some of the issues that came with the original release.

      First off, this patch does NOT address the following things:
    • It does NOT fix the consta-spawn problem, which obviously then does NOT fix the gren-spawning, C4-spawning, etc.

    • It does NOT fix player balance - we tweaked some settings a bit but we need more testing and time to really fix those up proper. keep the community feedback coming, every little bit helps!

      • Here are some things it DOES address:
    • Crash bugs. We have fixed at least 4 separate server and client crash bugs.

    • Network and local latency problems - fixed as much as was possible from the code. We have to scale back our maps a little in the update to really get the wailing net perf we want.

    • Fixed outrageously huge blood effect.

    • Spectator mode! Type to each other, keep an eye on your deployment timer, use the chase cam, etc.

      • We will have another patch to fix spawning issue VERY soon.

    Head to their files section for a list of available download sites.
